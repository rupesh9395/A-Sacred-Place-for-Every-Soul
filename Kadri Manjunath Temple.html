Kadri Manjunath Temple

Introduction

Kadri Manjunath Temple, located in Mangaluru, Karnataka, is one of the oldest and most revered temples in South India. Dedicated to Lord Manjunatha (a form of Shiva), the temple is a unique blend of Hindu and Buddhist traditions, reflecting centuries of spiritual evolution.

Location and Geography

The temple is situated on Kadri Hill Road in the Kadri area of Mangaluru. Surrounded by lush greenery and natural springs, it offers a serene environment for worship and meditation. The temple is easily accessible from the city center and nearby coastal regions.

Historical Background

Originally a Buddhist monastery known as Kadri Vihara, the temple dates back to the 10th or 11th century CE. It was later transformed into a Hindu temple during the 14th century. Inscriptions from the Alupa dynasty and references in the Bharadvaja Samhita highlight its ancient significance.

Architecture

The temple showcases Dravidian and Tuluva architectural styles. It features intricately carved stone pillars, bronze idols, and a sanctum housing the Shiva Linga. The temple also includes shrines for Ganesha, Durga, Shani Dev, and several Buddhist figures like Lokeshwar and Avalokiteshwar.

Deity and Worship

Lord Manjunatha is worshipped as a Shiva Linga, with rituals performed by Shivalli Madhva Brahmins. Devotees also honor Goddess Durga and other deities. The temple is known for its inclusive spiritual practices and daily poojas that blend Shaiva and Tantric traditions.

Temple Administration

Managed by the Kadri Temple Trust, the temple oversees daily rituals, festival arrangements, and pilgrim services. It also supports cultural preservation and eco-conscious initiatives.
