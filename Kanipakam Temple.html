Kapaleeshwarar Temple

Introduction

Kapaleeshwarar Temple, located in Mylapore, Chennai, Tamil Nadu, is a revered Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is a vibrant center of Shaivite worship and a cultural landmark known for its Dravidian architecture and mythological significance.

Location and Geography

The temple stands in the heart of Mylapore, one of Chennai’s oldest neighborhoods. Surrounded by bustling streets and traditional markets, it offers a spiritual oasis amidst urban life. The temple complex is close to the Bay of Bengal and is easily accessible from all parts of the city.

Historical Background

Originally built by the Pallavas in the 7th century CE, the temple was destroyed by the Portuguese in the 16th century. It was later rebuilt by the Vijayanagara kings in its current location. The temple is mentioned in the Tevaram hymns by Tamil saint poets, making it one of the Paadal Petra Sthalams.

Architecture

The temple showcases classic Dravidian architecture with a towering 37-meter gopuram adorned with colorful sculptures. The complex includes shrines for Kapaleeshwarar (Shiva) and Karpagambal (Parvati), along with mandapams, pillared halls, and a sacred tank.

Deity and Worship

Lord Shiva is worshipped as Kapaleeshwarar in the form of a lingam, and Goddess Parvati as Karpagambal. The temple follows Shaiva Agama traditions, with rituals performed by Tamil Brahmin priests. Devotees seek blessings for health, prosperity, and spiritual growth.

Temple Administration

The temple is managed by the Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Department of the Government of Tamil Nadu. It oversees daily operations, festivals, donations, and conservation efforts.

Rituals and Daily Schedule

Six daily rituals are performed from 5:30 AM to 10:00 PM, including Abhishekam, Alankaram, and Deeparadhana. Special poojas are held on Pradosham, Pournami, and other auspicious days.

Festivals

  • Arupathimoovar Festival – Celebrates the 63 Nayanmars with a grand procession in the Tamil month of Panguni.
  • Brahmotsavam – A ten-day annual festival with chariot processions and cultural events.
  • Mahashivaratri – Observed with night-long prayers and special rituals.

Legends and Mythology

According to legend, Goddess Parvati was cursed to become a peahen and performed penance at this site to regain her divine form. The name “Mylapore” derives from “Mayil” (peacock in Tamil). The temple is also associated with Lord Muruga receiving the divine spear (Vel) from his mother here.

Environmental and Cultural Initiatives

The temple promotes eco-friendly practices and supports classical music and dance performances, especially during festivals. It serves as a hub for Tamil culture and spirituality.

Security and Crowd Management

Surveillance systems, regulated entry, and crowd control measures ensure safety during peak hours and festivals. Volunteers assist with darshan queues and prasadam distribution.

Temple Timings

  • Morning: 5:30 AM – 12:00 PM
  • Evening: 4:00 PM – 9:30 PM

Accommodation and Facilities

Pilgrims can find lodging in nearby hotels and guesthouses. The temple offers prasadam counters, donation services, and guided tours during festivals.

Transportation

The temple is easily accessible by road and rail. The nearest railway station is Chennai MRTS Mylapore, and the closest airport is Chennai International Airport. Local buses and auto-rickshaws connect the temple to major city hubs.

Conclusion

Kapaleeshwarar Temple is a timeless symbol of devotion, mythology, and Tamil heritage. Its spiritual ambiance, architectural beauty, and vibrant festivals make it a cherished destination for devotees and cultural enthusiasts alike.
