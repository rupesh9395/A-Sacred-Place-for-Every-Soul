Srisailam Mallikarjuna Temple

Introduction

Srisailam Mallikarjuna Temple, also known as Sri Bhramaramba Mallikarjuna Swamy Varla Devasthanam, is a revered Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati. Located in the Nallamala Hills of Andhra Pradesh, it is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and one of the eighteen Shakti Peethas, making it a unique confluence of Shaivism and Shaktism.

Location and Geography

The temple is situated in Srisailam, Kurnool district, on the banks of the Krishna River. Nestled within the dense forests of the Nallamala range, the temple sits at an elevation of about 457 meters, offering a serene and spiritually charged environment.

Historical Background

The temple has ancient roots, with references in the Mahabharata, Skanda Purana, and Shiva Purana. Inscriptions from the Satavahana dynasty (2nd century CE) and later contributions from the Chalukyas, Kakatiyas, and Vijayanagara rulers highlight its historical significance. Saints like Adi Shankaracharya and Sri Rama are believed to have visited this sacred site.

Architecture

Built in the Dravidian style, the temple complex spans over two hectares and features four towering gopurams. The sanctum houses the Jyotirlinga of Lord Mallikarjuna and the shrine of Goddess Bhramaramba. The Mukha Mandapa, with intricately carved pillars, and the Sahasra Linga (thousand lingas) are architectural highlights.

Deity and Worship

Lord Shiva is worshipped as Mallikarjuna, and Goddess Parvati as Bhramaramba. The temple follows Shaiva Agama traditions, with rituals performed by Vedic scholars. Devotees seek blessings for spiritual liberation, health, and prosperity.

Temple Administration

Managed by the Srisaila Devasthanam Board under the Andhra Pradesh Endowments Department, the temple oversees daily rituals, festivals, donations, and pilgrim services. It also operates charitable trusts and educational institutions.

Rituals and Daily Schedule

Daily rituals include Suprabhata Seva, Abhishekam, Archana, and Ekantha Seva. Special poojas like Rudrabhishekam and Paroksha Seva are available for online booking. The temple opens from 4:30 AM to 10:00 PM.

Festivals

  • Mahashivaratri – Celebrated with night-long prayers, abhishekams, and cultural programs.
  • Kumbhabhishekam – A grand consecration ceremony held periodically.
  • Navaratri – Dedicated to Goddess Bhramaramba with special decorations and rituals.

Legends and Mythology

According to legend, Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati stayed in Srisailam to pacify their son Kartikeya. The temple is named after the jasmine (Mallika) flowers used in worship. It is also believed that the Pandavas and Lord Rama installed lingas here during their pilgrimage.

Environmental and Cultural Initiatives

The temple promotes eco-friendly practices, including plastic bans and forest conservation. It supports classical music, dance, and Vedic education through Srisaila TV and cultural festivals.

Security and Crowd Management

Surveillance systems, biometric entry, and regulated darshan slots ensure safety and smooth pilgrim flow. Volunteers assist with prasadam distribution and queue management during peak seasons.

Temple Timings

  • Morning: 4:30 AM – 1:00 PM
  • Evening: 3:00 PM – 10:00 PM

Accommodation and Facilities

Comfortable AC/Non-AC rooms, cottages, and guesthouses are available for pilgrims. Facilities include prasadam counters, medical aid, Anna Prasadam (free meals), and online booking for rooms and sevas.

Transportation

Srisailam is accessible by road from Hyderabad, Kurnool, and Nandyal. The nearest railway station is Markapur Road, and the closest airport is Rajiv Gandhi International Airport in Hyderabad. APSRTC buses and taxis operate regularly.

Conclusion

Srisailam Mallikarjuna Temple is a divine confluence of devotion, mythology, and natural beauty. Its status as both a Jyotirlinga and Shakti Peetha makes it a spiritually potent destination for seekers and devotees across India.
